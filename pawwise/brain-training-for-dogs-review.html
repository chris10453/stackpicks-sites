Verdict up front: Brain Training for Dogs is the program we recommend to most owners. It's force-free, it's a one-time payment instead of a subscription, and the "games" format keeps both you and your dog engaged. It's not video-based, so if you strongly prefer watching over reading, you may be happier with a video library instead.

Quick take: Best for owners who want a gentle, science-friendly method they own forever — and who don't mind lessons delivered as written steps with photos. 60-day money-back guarantee makes it low-risk to try.

What it is

Created by Adrienne Farricelli, a certified professional dog trainer, the program teaches obedience and fixes problem behaviors through a progression of mental-stimulation games. The idea is simple: a dog whose brain is worked is calmer, more focused, and far less likely to bark, chew, or dig out of boredom. You move through seven "schools" from easy to advanced, plus a troubleshooting section for specific problems.

What you get

  • The seven-module games course (from beginner to "Einstein")
  • A behavior-problem library — barking, biting, pulling, jumping, digging, and more
  • Access to a members' area where you can post questions
  • Obedience training basics as a foundation

Who it's for

Great for pet owners with a normal, healthy dog who want to fix everyday behavior and build a stronger bond — especially bored, high-energy, or mildly anxious dogs that thrive on mental work. It's not a substitute for a qualified behaviorist if your dog shows real aggression or severe fear.

Price & guarantee

It's a one-time payment (typically around $47) with a 60-day money-back guarantee, which is unusually generous and means you can actually work through the material and still get a refund if it doesn't help.
